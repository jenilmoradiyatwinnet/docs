    Top Paid shows apps that users pay for before downloading or accessing.

    Use this view to understand where users are willing to spend money.

    | Use case | What to look for |
    | --- | --- |
    | Premium app research | Apps that stay consistently ranked |
    | Pricing opportunity | Categories where paid apps perform well |
    | Competitor analysis | Paid competitors with strong ratings and installs |
    | Market validation | Countries where users purchase paid apps |
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Grossing">
    Grossing shows apps that are performing strongly from a revenue perspective.

    This is useful for understanding monetization-heavy apps, subscription models, in-app purchases, and high-value categories.

    <Info>
      Grossing rankings are useful when your goal is revenue research, monetization strategy, or competitor benchmarking.
    </Info>

    | Signal | What it can indicate |
    | --- | --- |
    | High grossing rank | Strong revenue or monetization performance |
    | Strong rating | Better user trust and product quality |
    | Large install base | Wide market reach |
    | Category position | Revenue opportunity inside a specific category |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How to use Market Trends

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose ranking type">
    Select Top Free, Top Paid, or Grossing based on what you want to analyze.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select country">
    Choose a country to understand region-specific app performance.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pick app category">
    Select categories such as finance, business, shopping, entertainment, tools, or lifestyle.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review app cards">
    Compare apps by name, rating, installs, ranking, and performance signals.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find opportunities">
    Use the insights to identify competitors, trends, user demand, and market gaps.
  </Step>
</Steps>
