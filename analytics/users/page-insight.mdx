## How to use Page Insights

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select date range">
    Choose Today, Yesterday, 7 Days, or a custom date range based on the period you want to analyze.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose country">
    Select All countries or filter by a specific country to compare page performance by market.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Switch between Screen and Fragment">
    Use the **Screen** tab for full page or activity-level performance. Use the **Fragment** tab for smaller app sections or component-level views.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review top pages">
    Check total visits, active users, average page views, and remaining users to understand which screens are performing well.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use average page view card">
    Review the average number of pages viewed per user to understand whether users are exploring more pages or leaving quickly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common use cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Find most visited pages" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Use the Most Visited Screens section to identify which screens or fragments users open most often.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Compare screen and fragment performance" icon="layer-group">
    Switch between Screen and Fragment tabs to understand whether users engage more with full pages or specific app sections.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Measure browsing depth" icon="chart-simple">
    Use Average Pages and Average Page Views to understand how deeply users explore your app or website.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Find drop-off areas" icon="arrow-trend-down">
    If a page has high visits but low remaining users, it may indicate users are leaving after that screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Analyze country-wise behavior" icon="earth-asia">
    Use the country filter to compare page engagement across different regions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
