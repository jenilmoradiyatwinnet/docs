<h2 id="roles-and-permissions" className="sr-only">
  Roles and permissions
</h2>

Use **Roles and Permissions** to control who can access your Twinalyze organization and what each person can do.

Membership is managed at the organization level. Invite members by email, assign the appropriate role, and configure access to specific Twinalyze sections when more detailed control is required.

---

## Role capabilities

Twinalyze provides four organization roles: **Owner**, **Admin**, **Analyst**, and **Viewer**.

| Organization role | Primary responsibility | Can invite | Can change roles | Can remove |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Owner** | Full organization ownership and control | Admin, Analyst, Viewer | Admin, Analyst, Viewer | Admin, Analyst, Viewer |
| **Admin** | Manage members, projects, settings, and permissions | Analyst, Viewer | Analyst, Viewer | Analyst, Viewer |
| **Analyst** | Analyze users, events, reports, and product performance | No | No | No |
| **Viewer** | Review permitted dashboards and reports without making changes | No | No | No |

Feature access can be further controlled using **Menu Group**, **Main Menu**, and **Sub Menu** permissions.

### Owner

The Owner has the highest level of access and is responsible for the organization.

Keep the number of Owners limited to people who require complete control over organization members, projects, settings, and permissions.

### Admin

Admins manage day-to-day organization operations.

They can manage projects, invite team members, update supported roles, configure permissions, and maintain organization settings.

### Analyst

Analysts work with product and user data.

Use this role for team members who need to analyze dashboards, reports, users, events, engagement, retention, and product performance.

### Viewer

Viewers have limited, read-only access.

Use this role for stakeholders, clients, or team members who only need to review the dashboards and reports available to them.

## Manage members

Open the **Users** page to review organization members and pending invitations.

| Member view | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **All Users** | Members who have accepted their invitation and joined the organization. |
| **Pending User** | Invitations that have been sent but have not yet been accepted. |

<Frame>
  ![Twinalyze users and invitation management page](/images/user_permission.png)
</Frame>

From this page, authorized users can invite members, review pending invitations, update roles, and remove members.

## Invite a member

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Open the Users page">
    Open your Twinalyze organization and go to the **Users** page.

    This page displays existing organization members and pending invitations.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Invite User">
    Click the **Invite User** button to open the invitation form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the email address">
    Enter the email address of the person you want to invite.

    Use the email address they will use to access Twinalyze.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Select the role that matches the member's responsibilities:

    - **Owner** for complete organization control
    - **Admin** for managing members, projects, and settings
    - **Analyst** for working with analytics and reports
    - **Viewer** for read-only access
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Invite** to send the organization invitation.

    Twinalyze sends an invitation to the entered email address.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the pending invitation">
    Open the **Pending User** tab.

    The invited member remains in this section until they accept the invitation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Change a member's role

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Open All Users">
    Go to the **Users** page and open the **All Users** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the member">
    Find the member whose role you want to update.

    Use the available search or filtering options when the organization contains many members.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the role selector">
    Click the current role shown for the member.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose the new role">
    Select **Admin**, **Analyst**, or **Viewer**, depending on the access the member requires.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the change">
    Save or confirm the role update.

    The member's access is updated according to the selected role and its assigned permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Revoke a pending invitation

Revoke an invitation when it was sent to the wrong email address or when the person no longer requires access.

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Open Pending User">
    Go to the **Users** page and open the **Pending User** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the invitation">
    Find the pending invitation you want to cancel.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the invitation actions">
    Open the available actions for the pending user.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Revoke the invitation">
    Select **Revoke Invitation** and confirm the action.

    The pending invitation is removed and can no longer be used to join the organization.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Remove a member

Removing a member revokes their access to the Twinalyze organization.

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Open All Users">
    Go to the **Users** page and open the **All Users** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the member">
    Find the member you want to remove from the organization.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open member actions">
    Open the available actions for that member.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Remove User">
    Select **Remove User** from the available options.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm removal">
    Review the confirmation message and remove the member.

    The user immediately loses access to the organization and its projects.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configure feature permissions

Roles define a member's general responsibilities, while feature permissions control which areas of Twinalyze each role can access.

Permissions are organized into three levels.

| Permission level | What it controls | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Menu Group** | A complete product area in Twinalyze | Users |
| **Main Menu** | A page inside a Menu Group | Page Insights |
| **Sub Menu** | A nested page, report, or action | Detailed report |

Access normally follows this hierarchy:

**Menu Group → Main Menu → Sub Menu**

A role may require access to the parent Menu Group and Main Menu before it can access a Sub Menu.

## Give a role access

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Choose a permission level">
    Open **Menu Group**, **Main Menu**, or **Sub Menu**, depending on the level of access you want to configure.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select the role">
    Choose **Owner**, **Admin**, **Analyst**, or **Viewer** from the Role Type selector.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Find the feature">
    Use the search field or available filters to find the required menu, page, report, or feature.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the permission editor">
    Click the pencil icon for the permission you want to update.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign the role">
    Select the roles that should be allowed to access the feature.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable the permission">
    Make sure the permission status is active.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the changes">
    Save the permission configuration.

    Members assigned to the selected role can now access the feature according to the configured hierarchy.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permission controls

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Role Type** | Displays permissions assigned to the selected role. |
| **Status** | Shows whether a permission is active or inactive. |
| **Search** | Finds a Menu Group, Main Menu, Sub Menu, or page by name. |
| **Pencil icon** | Opens the permission editor. |
| **Status toggle** | Enables or disables the selected permission. |
| **Save** | Applies the updated role permission configuration. |

## What do the controls mean?

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Role Type** | Shows permissions assigned to a selected role. |
| **Status** | Shows active or inactive permissions. |
| **Search** | Finds a menu or page by name. |
| **Pencil icon** | Opens the permission editor. |
| **Status toggle** | Enables or disables the permission. |
